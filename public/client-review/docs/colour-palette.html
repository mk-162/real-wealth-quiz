Colour Palette and Tokens

Primary, secondary, and neutral colours with usage guidance.

The palette is a warm, two-temperature system. Teal carries the brand's depth and trust; orange carries its warmth and energy. Navy anchors dark surfaces. Gold is a quiet tertiary accent. Stone and ink are the neutrals that do the real work.

---

Primary

Brand Teal — #0c7372

The single most-used brand colour. Use for:

  • Primary heading colour on light backgrounds
  • Secondary CTAs and interactive links
  • Icon colour in feature blocks
  • The teal end of the hero gradient

Pairs cleanly with white (5.0:1 contrast at body size) and with paper-off-white neutrals. Avoid pairing with orange directly (vibration) — use navy or ink between them.

Brand Orange — #ff6801

The warmth-and-activity colour. Use for:

  • Primary CTAs ("Book a 20-minute call")
  • Active states and selected options
  • The accent bar on provocation cards
  • Hover highlights

Orange on white fails WCAG AA at body size (3.5:1). Use orange as border, icon, or large-display colour only — not as body text on white. For CTAs, use white text on orange (4.6:1) which passes AA.

---

Secondary

Navy — #333566

Deep, authoritative. Use for:

  • Footer background
  • Dense type blocks
  • Dark mode surfaces if ever required
  • The darker end of optional hero variations

Pairs with white at 12.2:1 — very high contrast, excellent for long-form reading.

Gold — #ffd33a

Tertiary accent. Use sparingly:

  • Small badges ("new," "recommended")
  • Illustrative chart highlights
  • Occasional divider colour

Do not use gold as a CTA colour — it competes with orange and weakens the hierarchy.

---

Neutrals

Ink — #353535

Primary body text. Do not use pure black. 10.9:1 on white; exceeds AAA.

Stone — #b2b2b2

Secondary text (captions, meta info), dividers, placeholder text, disabled states. Do not use for body copy — fails AA at body size.

Paper — #ffffff

Primary background. Not an off-white. Keep it clean.

---

Gradients

Teal gradient (hero)

background: linear-gradient(135deg, #0c7372 5%, rgb(17,160,159) 32%);

The default brand-hero gradient. Use on:

  • Landing-page hero backdrop
  • Section dividers that need drama
  • Large surfaces where a flat colour would feel heavy

Apply white text (min 24px) over this gradient.

Orange gradient (energy)

background: linear-gradient(140deg, #ff6801 2%, rgb(255,169,99) 44%);

Use sparingly on:

  • Protection or action-oriented sections
  • Feature callout surfaces
  • Occasional illustrated backgrounds

Apply white text (min 24px, bold) over this gradient. Body text over orange is not recommended.

---

Do and do not

Do use orange sparingly — one CTA per screen maximum.

Do use teal as the workhorse brand colour — it should appear on almost every screen.

Do test any new colour combination against WCAG AA before shipping.

Do not mix orange and gold in the same component — they flatten each other.

Do not use orange as body text on white.

Do not introduce additional accent colours without a reason. The palette is intentionally limited.

Do not use black (#000). Always use ink #353535 — the brand's deepest neutral.

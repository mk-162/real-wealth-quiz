# S6

## Goal 1 — Complete the sale at the current BADR rate

status: green
capacity: "The BADR rate is 14% now and rises to 18% from April 2026. On a £1m qualifying gain, the difference between the two rates is £40,000 of tax. The timeline is tight but achievable if the process begins now."
rationale: "The goal is within reach — and the financial value of achieving it is specific and large enough to treat the timeline as a constraint, not a preference."

---

## Goal 2 — Generate £80,000 a year in retirement

status: green
capacity: "Post-exit liquid wealth, projected state pension, and investment income together suggest this level of spending is sustainable at a 4% drawdown rate. The sequencing — which pot is drawn from first — affects the tax position."
rationale: "Achievable. The income planning conversation is the natural next step after the exit structure is resolved."

---

## Goal 3 — Set up a legacy for grandchildren

status: amber
capacity: "Post-exit estate is projected to exceed the nil-rate band significantly. Annual gifting allowances and trust structures are available and effective — but none are currently in place. The 7-year clock needs to start."
rationale: "The goal is achievable, but delayed starts cost real money. Each year the gifting clock is not running is a year the 7-year exemption cannot accrue."

---

## Goal 4 — Resolve succession — family versus external buyer

status: amber
capacity: "The decision has not been made. An unresolved succession question typically adds 6–12 months to the sale timeline and can reduce the price — buyers price uncertainty."
rationale: "Not a financial question yet, but it will become one. The earlier the conversation, the more options remain on the table."

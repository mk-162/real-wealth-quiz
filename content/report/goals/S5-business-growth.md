# Goal 1 — Exit the business at 60 for £500,000 net or more

status: red
capacity: "No documented exit plan in place. No two-year preparation period started. Without a plan, buyers discount the price, the tax structure defaults to a worse outcome, and the timeline runs longer than expected. Most business sales take 18–24 months from decision to completion."
rationale: "This goal is at risk — not because the business isn't valuable, but because the plan to realise that value doesn't exist yet."

---

# Goal 2 — Pay off the mortgage by 62

status: green
capacity: "Serviceable at the current repayment rate and business income level. On track assuming the business income remains stable."
rationale: "On track — with the caveat that the mortgage sits alongside business income risk. A lean year in the business would be felt here."

---

# Goal 3 — Fund children through university

status: amber
capacity: "Possible, but dependent on business cash flow rather than a ring-fenced savings vehicle. A personal ISA or investment account provides the certainty the business income cannot."
rationale: "Achievable, but fragile. Building a personal savings structure alongside the business changes the risk profile of this goal."

---

# Goal 4 — Achieve financial independence from the business

status: red
capacity: "The business is 45% of net worth and the primary source of income. Personal wealth built outside the business is growing slowly relative to business value. Independence — the ability to walk away without financial compromise — is not yet within reach."
rationale: "The most important goal on the page, and the hardest. The pension is the most tax-efficient way to build personal wealth alongside the business — and it is currently underused."

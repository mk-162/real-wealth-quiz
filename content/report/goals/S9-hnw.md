# Goal 1 — Leave a multi-generational legacy

status: red
capacity: "£6m+ estate — more than adequate in size. The constraint is inheritance tax. At current estate value, the projected liability is approximately £1.9m before any planning. Without structured intervention, that is the amount HMRC receives before the family inherits anything. Trusts, timed gifting, and Business Relief portfolios are the tools that change this number — but they take years to work properly."
rationale: "This is the single most important planning conversation on the page. Every year without a plan costs real money."

---

# Goal 2 — Fund meaningful philanthropy

status: green
capacity: "At a total spend rate of 2.1% of wealth, there is substantial capacity for charitable giving alongside retirement income. Structuring philanthropy through a donor-advised fund or charitable trust also reduces the inheritance tax exposure — the two goals reinforce each other."
rationale: "Well within reach, and the tax structuring around it is complementary to the IHT planning."

---

# Goal 3 — Support adult children and grandchildren during your lifetime

status: green
capacity: "Annual gifting allowances are substantially underused. Lifetime transfers of up to £3,000 per year are immediately exempt from IHT. Larger gifts start the 7-year clock — and at this estate level, running that clock early is one of the most valuable decisions available."
rationale: "The capacity is there. The planning insight is to start the gifting clock earlier rather than later — each year of inaction is a year that cannot be recovered."

---

# Goal 4 — Maintain current lifestyle indefinitely

status: green
capacity: "At a 2.1% spend-to-wealth ratio, the current lifestyle is sustainable under essentially any plausible market scenario. This goal is not the planning challenge — it is the baseline assumption everything else is built on top of."
rationale: "Fully secured. The planning energy belongs on legacy structure and inheritance tax, not income sustainability."

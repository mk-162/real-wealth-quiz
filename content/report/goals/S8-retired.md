# S8

## Goal 1 — Remain financially independent at home

status: amber
capacity: "Drawdown runway to around age 91 at current spend — a 4-year gap against a 95-year planning horizon. Home equity is an available reserve the plan does not currently rely on. Modest adjustments to the drawdown rate or withdrawal mix close most of the gap without significant lifestyle change."
rationale: "Manageable. The conversation is about the shape of the drawdown plan — not a crisis."

---

## Goal 2 — Help grandchildren financially

status: amber
capacity: "Gifting is possible at the current income level. The constraint is the longevity risk — generous gifting now competes with the income runway needed later. The 7-year inheritance tax clock is worth running where possible."
rationale: "Achievable in moderation. A planner can help define what is prudent to give without compromising the long-term position."

---

## Goal 3 — Leave something meaningful to children

status: amber
capacity: "Below the inheritance tax threshold at current estate value — no tax drag on what the children receive. The longevity risk is the main variable: a longer life is a good outcome but does affect how much remains."
rationale: "The estate is in good shape for this goal. The risk is not tax — it is longevity eroding the pot more than expected."

---

## Goal 4 — Manage healthcare changes without burdening the family

status: red
capacity: "No Lasting Power of Attorney in place — the most urgent legal gap at this stage. Without it, no family member can legally manage financial or healthcare decisions if capacity is lost. Care costs at current residential rates (£60,000–£100,000 a year) can erode the estate significantly without prior planning."
rationale: "Two things to do: register both LPAs, and spend 30 minutes understanding the care-cost landscape. Neither is expensive — and both matter enormously if the situation changes."

# S1 — Early Accumulator

## Zone: green (typical ~105%)
"On track for a normal retirement at 65 — time is doing the work. The biggest lever right now is making sure your employer pension match is fully taken."

## Zone: amber (if contributions fall below threshold)
"Slightly behind where time alone would put you. Increasing your contribution by 2–3% now costs less than you'd expect and compounds significantly over 37 years."

---

# S2 — Mass-Affluent Mid-Career

## Zone: amber (typical ~88%)
"Around £80,000 behind target at your chosen retirement age. Lifting your pension contribution by 3% today closes most of the gap by the time you get there."

## Zone: red (if contribution rate is very low or estate is below benchmark)
"A material gap against target — but at 42, it is still entirely closeable. Pension contribution rate is the most powerful lever available at your stage."

## Zone: green (if contributions are above age-target and estate above £500k)
"On track. The question shifts from building the pot to making sure the right documents are in place for the people who depend on it."

---

# S3 — High-Earner Mid-Career

## Zone: green (typical ~124%)
"Ahead of target for retirement at 60. Your biggest unused lever is not investment returns — it is the tax paid on income in the £100,000–£125,140 band each year."

## Zone: blue (if estate is above £2m and contributions are optimised)
"Comfortably ahead. The conversation shifts from accumulation to protecting what you have built — and reducing the inheritance tax bill that is starting to appear on the horizon."

---

# S4 — Senior Professional

## Zone: blue (typical ~142%)
"Comfortably ahead of target. The focus has shifted from building the pot to protecting it from inheritance tax and drawing from it in the most efficient order."

## Zone: green (if lower end of senior professional band)
"On track for retirement at 60. The most valuable conversation now is about drawdown sequencing and the inheritance tax exposure — not further accumulation."

---

# S5 — Business Owner Growth

## Zone: amber (typical ~78%, business excluded from projection)
"Score depends almost entirely on what happens to the business. A clean exit at fair value adds roughly 30 points to this number — no exit, or a distressed one, subtracts 20."

## Zone: red (if pension is very small and no personal savings)
"Below target without accounting for a business sale. Building personal wealth alongside the business — not instead of it — is the change that moves this score."

---

# S6 — Business Owner Exit

## Zone: green (typical ~128%)
"On track if the sale executes at the expected value. The number that matters most now is not the projected balance — it is the tax structure on the day the contracts are signed."

## Zone: blue (if business is high-value and BADR planning is in place)
"Well ahead of target once the exit is complete. The planning story is about what happens to the proceeds — not whether there will be enough."

---

# S7 — Pre-Retiree Affluent

## Zone: green (typical ~135%)
"Comfortably on track for retirement at 65. The conversation shifts from accumulation to sequencing — which pot you draw from first, and when the state pension fits into the picture."

## Zone: blue (if estate is large and pension is well-funded)
"Well ahead. The question is not whether the plan delivers — it is whether what remains after retirement goes to the people you intend, and in what proportion."

---

# S8 — Retired (gauge reframes to lifetime coverage %)

## Zone: amber (typical ~91% of lifetime covered)
"Funds projected to last to around age 91 at your current spend — a 4-year gap against a 95-year planning horizon. A modest adjustment or a single drawdown decision closes it."

## Zone: green (if drawdown rate is sustainable with margin)
"Sustainable at current spend through to around 94. The gap against a 95-year horizon is small — the more pressing conversation is LPA and the 2027 pension IHT change."

## Zone: red (if drawdown rate is high relative to pot)
"At the current spend rate, the projection runs short of the planning horizon. There are a handful of ways to address this — and most of them are smaller changes than you might expect."

---

# S9 — HNW Multi-Generational

## Zone: blue (typical ~240%)
"Far above what you need. Financial sustainability is not in question. The chart that matters most to your family is the one showing what they inherit — and what the inheritance tax bill will be."

## Zone: blue (alternative framing for lower S9 scores, e.g. 160%)
"Well ahead of any reasonable retirement target. The planning challenge has shifted entirely — it is not about building the pot, it is about the structure that determines what the family keeps."

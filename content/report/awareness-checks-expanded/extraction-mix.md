# Business owner extraction mix — the tax review that's often overdue

For most owner-managed companies, the most tax-efficient way to take money out of the business is a combination of salary, dividends, and employer pension contributions — calibrated to the current year's tax rates and the owner's personal circumstances. The classic structure sets salary at around the NI threshold (currently £12,570 per year), takes dividends up to the basic-rate band, and uses employer pension contributions for anything above that. On £100,000 of extraction, the difference between this approach and pure salary can be £15,000–£20,000 of tax per year.

The problem is that the optimum mix shifts every tax year, and the adjustment is easy to defer. The dividend allowance dropped from £2,000 to £1,000 in April 2023 and to £500 in April 2024, which changes the maths. Corporation tax rose to 25% for profits above £250,000 in April 2023. A strategy that was well-optimised three years ago may no longer be. "My accountant handles it" is often accurate but doesn't always mean the review has happened recently.

This is not a conversation to have over the top of your accountant — it is one to have with them. What we typically find is that combining the accountant's view on the corporate structure with a planner's view on the personal tax position reveals gaps neither party was seeing in isolation. In our experience the extraction review is where business-owner clients most commonly find savings they weren't expecting, and it is usually the first agenda item of a planning engagement.

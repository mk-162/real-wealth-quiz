# Care funding — the cost most families have not planned for

Long-term residential care in the UK typically costs £60,000–£100,000 per year, depending on the type of care required and the location. It is not free at the point of use for most families. Under the means-test rules in England, if your total assets exceed £23,250 — including the value of a property you no longer occupy — you are expected to fund the full cost of your own care. The family home is disregarded only while a spouse, civil partner, or dependent continues to live there; once the property is empty, its value counts.

The average stay in a residential care setting is around 30 months, but that average conceals a very wide range. A significant minority of residents stay for five years or more. At £90,000 per year, five years of care costs £450,000 — an amount that can substantially reduce the estate a family was expecting to pass on. A planned approach to care funding — immediate-needs annuities, property structures, pre-funded care bonds — can preserve a meaningful portion of that cost. In our experience, families who plan the care question early typically preserve £100,000–£300,000 more of the estate than those who do not.

This is not a comfortable conversation to initiate, which is why it tends to happen too late. For anyone over 60, or for households with elderly parents whose care needs may eventually fall partly on the family, a basic understanding of the thresholds, the options, and the cost landscape is worth having now — before the conversation is forced by circumstances.

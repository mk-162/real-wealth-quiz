# Independent versus restricted advice — the distinction most people haven't been told about

In UK financial services, a "financial adviser" can be either independent or restricted, and the distinction affects the scope of what they can recommend. An independent adviser can consider the whole of market — any regulated product, provider, or structure available. A restricted adviser is limited to a defined range, which can mean anything from "most of the market, excluding one or two product types" to "products from a single provider or group." The FCA requires advisers to disclose their status at the start of any advice relationship, but the disclosure can be buried in initial paperwork that many clients do not read closely.

Around 60% of UK advice firms are restricted rather than independent. This is not automatically a problem. Some restricted firms are restricted in a narrow and largely irrelevant way — for example, restricted in the types of mortgages they can advise on, while unrestricted for investments and pensions, which is where most of the planning sits. But some restrictions are more material: a firm restricted to recommending products from a single provider or a single investment platform cannot access the pricing or terms available elsewhere in the market. In those cases the restriction can have a meaningful cost.

If you are currently receiving advice, the easiest way to check is to look at the initial disclosure document or client agreement — the restriction status will be stated there, usually in the first few pages. If the document is unclear, asking directly ("are you independent or restricted, and what does your restriction cover?") is a standard and entirely reasonable question. Real Wealth is an independent firm — which means we are required by the FCA to consider the whole of market, not a subset of it.

# Savings and investment tax efficiency — the allowances that most people under-use

Three annual allowances reduce the tax due on savings and investment returns: the £20,000 ISA allowance, the £3,000 capital gains tax annual exempt amount, and the £500 dividend allowance. Used individually they are modest. Used together across a couple with a shared strategy, they add up to £46,500 of tax-protected activity per year. Most couples use less than half of this combined capacity.

The compounding benefit of ISA sheltering tends to be underestimated. A £100,000 portfolio outside an ISA, growing at 6% per year, generates around £6,000 of growth annually — subject to CGT and dividend tax. Moving £20,000 a year inside an ISA over five years protects the whole portfolio from tax on future growth. The technique involved — selling assets outside the ISA and repurchasing them inside — is sometimes called "Bed and ISA," and it is one of the most common first-year conversations we have with new clients who have meaningful investments outside a wrapper. The CGT annual exempt amount helps absorb the gain on the disposal, and the timing can usually be managed to keep the disposal within the exempt threshold.

The allowances change regularly — the dividend allowance was cut from £2,000 to £500 between 2023 and 2024, meaning couples who set up a joint strategy more than two years ago may be working from out-of-date assumptions. A review of how the current mix sits against this year's figures is typically worth the time, particularly for households with assets across multiple wrappers and both partners' allowances potentially available.

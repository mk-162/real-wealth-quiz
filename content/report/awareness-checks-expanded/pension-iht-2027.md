# Pensions and inheritance tax from April 2027

For the past fifteen years, unused defined-contribution pension funds have sat outside the estate for inheritance tax purposes — which made pensions one of the most effective intergenerational wealth transfer vehicles available. The Autumn 2024 Budget announced that this position is expected to change from April 2027, when unused DC pension wealth will be brought inside the estate and subject to IHT in the normal way. Draft legislation is expected in 2025, and the detail will matter, but the direction of travel is clear.

This changes one of the most widely used planning rules of thumb: draw from ISAs and investments first, leave the pension to pass free of inheritance tax. If the 2027 rules take effect as announced, that sequencing will no longer hold for most families with large pension pots and estates above the nil-rate threshold. For a family with £500,000 in defined-contribution pensions and a £1.5m estate, the change could add £200,000 to the IHT bill if the drawdown order is not revisited.

The 2026–27 window — before the rules change — is where most of the relevant decisions sit. That includes reviewing drawdown sequencing, assessing whether pension wealth should be drawn down faster in the interim, and considering whether other IHT planning tools (gifting, trust structures, Business Relief investments) need to be accelerated. This is a live conversation for anyone with a large pension and a taxable estate, not a planning point for later.

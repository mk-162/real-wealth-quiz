# Life cover through work — the protection that ends when you leave

Life cover provided through an employer — typically a death-in-service benefit of 3–4 times salary — is a valuable benefit, but it exists only while you remain employed by that company. It cannot be taken with you when you leave, it does not apply during periods of redundancy or career transition, and many schemes reduce or withdraw cover at certain ages. The same applies to critical illness cover and income protection provided through group schemes.

For a family with dependants, a mortgage, and significant financial obligations, the gap between the cover provided by employment and the cover the family actually needs can be substantial. A £500,000 personal life policy and £50,000 per year of income protection for a healthy 40-year-old non-smoker typically costs under £50 a month combined — considerably less than most people assume when they first enquire. The premium is lower in early life when health is good and a long income protection period is still available; the same cover becomes more expensive, and potentially harder to obtain, if it is sought later after a health event.

The risk is not catastrophic when things are going well. It becomes real on the day employment ends — redundancy, a career change, a business venture — and the assumption that "I'm covered through work" is suddenly no longer true. Personal cover, set up while healthy, is portable, predictable, and not dependent on an employer's continued participation in a group scheme. A quick check of the exact terms and limits of existing group cover is often the starting point for understanding whether any personal cover is worth adding.

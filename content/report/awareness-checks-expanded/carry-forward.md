# Pension carry-forward — the three-year window most people don't use

The standard pension annual allowance is £60,000. But if you have been a member of a UK pension scheme in the previous three tax years and have not used your full allowance in those years, you can carry the unused amount forward and add it to the current year's limit. Depending on your circumstances, this can make a one-off contribution of up to around £200,000 possible in a single year.

For a higher-rate taxpayer, a £200,000 pension contribution using carry-forward can save approximately £80,000–£90,000 of income tax in that year alone. For a business owner with a profitable year, an employer pension contribution of the same size is deductible for corporation tax — saving an additional £50,000 at the 25% main rate. The two effects combined can make carry-forward one of the most tax-efficient single decisions available, typically far more so than any individual investment choice.

The window is rolling: each April, one year of carry-forward drops off the back and a new year opens at the front. Unused allowance from 2021/22 was available until 5 April 2025 — after which it was gone. The mechanism rewards planning ahead and penalises leaving it late. In our experience, many high-earners and business owners in a good year reach April having not used it, then discover they had the capacity to shelter a very large sum and the window has closed. The call is worth making in the autumn of a good year, not the spring.

# Pension consolidation — what four or more pots usually hides

Multiple workplace pension pots are common for anyone who has changed employer more than twice. The pensions industry calls this fragmentation; in practice it means that some portion of your retirement savings is in a scheme you left years ago, probably charging more than your current pension, and possibly with features you have never examined.

Old workplace schemes often carry annual charges of 1.0%–1.5%. Modern equivalents are typically 0.2%–0.5%. On a £50,000 pot, the difference between 1.5% and 0.3% in annual charges, compounded over twenty years, is roughly £20,000 of terminal value. Across multiple old pots the impact is proportionally larger. The average new client we work with identifies £40,000–£60,000 across workplace pensions they had largely forgotten about — sometimes significantly more. The discovery alone tends to change how the overall picture looks.

The decision is not always to consolidate everything. A small number of older schemes carry genuinely valuable safeguarded benefits — protected tax-free cash above the standard 25%, guaranteed annuity rates that can produce an income significantly higher than a drawdown strategy — and transferring out of those schemes would permanently lose those guarantees. That is why the analysis tends to be "consolidate the right ones" rather than "consolidate everything." The first step is usually getting a current valuation and a key features document for each old pot, which is worth doing regardless of what the consolidation decision ultimately turns out to be.

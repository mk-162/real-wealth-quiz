# Business Asset Disposal Relief — the rate is rising, and the timeline matters

Business Asset Disposal Relief gives a reduced CGT rate on qualifying business disposals up to a £1m lifetime limit. To qualify, you generally need to have held at least a 5% stake in the business and worked in it for at least two years. Until April 2025 the rate was 10%. The Autumn 2024 Budget confirmed it would rise to 14% from April 2025 and 18% from April 2026 — where it is currently expected to remain.

The tax difference across the rate steps is straightforward but material. On a £1m qualifying gain, BADR at 10% produced a £100,000 tax bill. At 14% it is £140,000. At 18% it is £180,000. The £80,000 difference between the 10% and 18% rates is decided entirely by the date of completion. Most business sales take 18–24 months from the initial decision to contracts exchanging — which means a business owner who has not yet started the process is likely completing into the 18% era regardless of when they begin.

The timing conversation is not simply "sell faster." Splitting consideration across two tax years, structuring earnout payments, or accelerating certain elements of the sale can all affect how much of the gain falls into a more favourable year. The question of whether to aim for the 14% window, the 18% window, or to split consideration is one most sellers have with their advisers later than is useful. If an exit is on the horizon within five years, it is worth modelling the tax position now rather than when heads of terms are on the table.

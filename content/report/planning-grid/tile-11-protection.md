# S1 — Early Accumulator

status: grey
note: "Not asked in detail — and less urgent with no dependants. Becomes important quickly when the picture changes."

---

# S2 — Mass-Affluent Mid-Career

status: red
note: "Work cover only — ends on the last day of employment. With a mortgage and dependants, a personal policy is the gap that matters most."

---

# S3 — High-Earner Mid-Career

status: red
note: "Likely under-covered for this income level. Work cover does not match the lifestyle it would need to replace."

---

# S4 — Senior Professional

status: amber
note: "Still relevant until retirement. Worth confirming that a personal policy is in place alongside any group benefit — not instead of it."

---

# S5 — Business Owner Growth

status: red
note: "Key-person cover and shareholder protection are typically absent at this stage. These are business continuity risks as much as personal ones."

---

# S6 — Business Owner Exit

status: amber
note: "Personal cover becomes less critical post-exit. Key-person protection for the business still matters in the 18–24 months before completion."

---

# S7 — Pre-Retiree Affluent

status: amber
note: "Winding down naturally as retirement approaches. Worth confirming what cover remains in place and whether it still matches what you actually need."

---

# S8 — Retired

status: grey
note: "Less relevant at this stage. Care-cost planning fulfils a similar function — the protection question reframes around longevity and capacity."

---

# S9 — HNW Multi-Generational

status: amber
note: "Personal cover may be in place — but at this estate level, the protection question is more about trust structures than traditional insurance policies."

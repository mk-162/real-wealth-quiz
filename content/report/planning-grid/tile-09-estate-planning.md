# S1 — Early Accumulator

status: grey
note: "No will or LPA likely — and less critical with no dependants. When the picture changes, this becomes urgent quickly."

---

# S2 — Mass-Affluent Mid-Career

status: red
note: "No current will with dependent children — the single most urgent document gap for a family at this stage. Intestacy gives children equal shares at 18."

---

# S3 — High-Earner Mid-Career

status: amber
note: "Will in place. LPA is the likely gap — the most common missing document for this age group. Both types should be registered."

---

# S4 — Senior Professional

status: amber
note: "Will present. LPA is the gap. At this age and income level, both documents should be in place, current, and known to the family."

---

# S5 — Business Owner Growth

status: red
note: "Will absent or old, and no shareholder agreement — two document risks that interact badly if something unexpected happens to you or a co-owner."

---

# S6 — Business Owner Exit

status: amber
note: "Will in place. LPA is the likely gap. At this age, both documents should be current and registered — the exit makes them more urgent, not less."

---

# S7 — Pre-Retiree Affluent

status: amber
note: "Both documents exist — but are they current? A will written before major asset changes can fail to reflect what you actually want."

---

# S8 — Retired

status: red
note: "LPA is the most critical document at this stage. Without it, no family member can legally manage your money or healthcare if capacity is lost."

---

# S9 — HNW Multi-Generational

status: amber
note: "Basic documents in place. At this estate level, the trust and gifting structure is the larger conversation — but the foundations need to be current first."
